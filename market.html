The Monthly Outdoor Market

One Saturday a month. Outdoor booths, morning light, and the whole beautiful mess of it.

The best kind of
Saturday morning.

Once a month, we open our outdoor space to vendors, makers, pickers, and the general public for a full-scale outdoor market. It's the kind of event that starts with coffee and ends with you hauling something unexpected — and wonderful — to your car.

Whether you're there to sell or just to wander, the market has a particular energy that's hard to describe and easy to love. Local vendors line up their tables, regulars show up early with cash in hand, and newcomers discover for the first time what it feels like to find the exact thing they didn't know they needed.

We keep it curated — not just a flea market, but a real gathering of people who care about quality, history, and the joy of the hunt. Come once and you'll start penciling in every date.

Market Details

When One Saturday per month — dates announced monthly via social media and our email list
Where Memory Lane Vintage Mall outdoor lot, Stillwater, Oklahoma
Hours Vendors set up from 8:00 AM – 10:00 AM. Market open to the public 10:00 AM – 5:00 PM
Who's There Antique dealers, vintage collectors, handmade makers, and local food vendors
Admission Free for shoppers. Vendor booth fee required to sell
Booth Fees 10x10 booth space — $50 · Single table or comparable display — $30
Rain Policy Light rain — we go anyway. Heavy weather — we'll let vendors know via email in advance

Who belongs at the market.

Our market draws a crowd that's genuinely looking for something real — not mass-produced, not generic. That means the vendors we invite should bring the same intention.

We welcome:

  • ◆ Vintage and antique sellers of all kinds
  • ◆ Handmade and artisan goods makers
  • ◆ Upcycled furniture and salvage sellers
  • ◆ Vintage clothing and textile vendors
  • ◆ Books, records, and paper goods
  • ◆ Small-batch food and beverage vendors (select markets)

Not sure if your goods are a good fit? Apply anyway and tell us about what you sell. We'd rather know about you than wonder.

"There's something about an outdoor market on a cool morning — the way people slow down, the way strangers start conversations over a table of old things. We built this market because we wanted more of that feeling in Stillwater."
